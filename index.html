About Me

I am an Experimental Perceptual-Motor Scientist with nearly a decade of experience using VR and AR to study human perception and locomotion. I received my PhD in Cogntive Science from Brown University in the Winter of 2021. My doctoral thesis implemented virtual reality in a 10x10m room to examine how humans walked through pre-generated crowd stimuli. Since Fall of 2020, I have been a post-doctoral researcher in the Human Movement Neuroscience Lab working with Jon Matthis. As a post-doc, I built a new experimental paradigm using a Unity & Qualisys based augmented reality ground plane to examine the relationship between eye movements and foot placement across complex terrain. You can find this project on GitHub.

In addition to my post-doctoral duties, I am leading the usability and product sides of The Free Motion Capture Project (FreeMoCap | freemocap.org). FreeMoCap takes modern advancements in computer vision and packages them to enable free 3D motion capture software for everyone!
